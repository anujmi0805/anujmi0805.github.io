What is XIRR?

XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) is a practical method to compute the annualized growth rate of investments that occur at irregular intervals. It helps investors understand real returns for SIPs or other staggered contributions, rather than assuming all money was invested at once.

Why XIRR Matters

Unlike CAGR, XIRR accounts for the exact dates of each investment, making it ideal for real-life investing scenarios. This ensures that the reported returns closely reflect the actual cash flow timing, giving a more realistic view of performance.

Example

Suppose you invest monthly through a SIP for five years. The XIRR Calculator will consider all the deposit dates and the final redemption value to provide an accurate annual growth rate, showing how your investments truly performed.

FAQs

What is XIRR used for?
XIRR is used to calculate returns when investments occur at different times, such as SIPs, staggered deposits, or irregular contributions. This helps in understanding true annualized returns.
Is XIRR better than CAGR?
For investments made at irregular intervals, XIRR is more accurate than CAGR because it considers the timing of each cash flow.
Can I use XIRR for SIPs?
Absolutely. XIRR is ideal for SIPs as it evaluates the exact timing of each installment and calculates a realistic annualized return.
Does XIRR include compounding?
Yes, compounding is inherently factored into the XIRR calculation since it annualizes returns over the investment period.
Can XIRR be negative?
Yes. If the final investment value is lower than the invested amount due to loss, XIRR will be negative, reflecting a negative annualized return.
What is the difference between IRR and XIRR?
IRR assumes equal intervals between cash flows, whereas XIRR accounts for exact dates, making it more precise for real-world investments.
Can XIRR handle multiple investments over time?
Yes. You can add multiple investments at varying times, and XIRR will calculate the effective annualized return across all cash flows.
Why should I use XIRR over CAGR for my portfolio?
If you make investments irregularly or through SIPs, XIRR provides a more realistic performance measurement, which helps in better planning and comparison with other investment options.
