Rule of 72

The Rule of 72 is a simple and popular formula used to estimate the number of years required to double an investment at a fixed annual return. By dividing 72 by the annual interest rate, you can quickly determine the approximate doubling time.

Calculate Doubling Time

Years to Double Investment

9.00 Years

Understanding the Rule of 72

Divide 72 by the annual interest rate (in %) to estimate how many years it will take to double your investment. For example, at 8% interest, it would take roughly 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years.

Applications of the Rule

Investors can use this rule to quickly understand growth potential for compounding investments, savings accounts, mutual funds, and fixed deposits.

Limitations

This is an approximation assuming constant returns. Fluctuating market conditions may slightly change actual results, but it’s a practical shortcut for planning purposes.

FAQs

What is the Rule of 72?
A formula to estimate the number of years it takes to double an investment at a fixed annual return.
How is it different from Rule of 114?
Rule of 114 estimates tripling time, Rule of 72 estimates doubling time.
Can it be used for any investment?
Yes, for savings accounts, mutual funds, fixed deposits, and other compounding investments.
Does it account for market fluctuations?
No, it assumes a fixed annual return.
Is it suitable for short-term investments?
Best for medium to long-term investments due to compounding.
Why use the Rule of 72?
It gives a quick estimate for planning long-term investment goals without complex calculations.
Can it replace full financial planning?
No, it’s a quick mental shortcut. Detailed planning is recommended.
