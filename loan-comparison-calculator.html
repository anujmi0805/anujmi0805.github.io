Why Compare Loans?

Comparing loans is essential to avoid overpaying. Even small differences in interest rates or tenures can impact total repayment significantly. Using this tool ensures you understand the financial implications of each loan option clearly.

What to Look For

When comparing loans, consider total repayment, EMI affordability, and interest paid. A lower EMI might look attractive but may increase the total interest burden if the tenure is longer. Balance short-term affordability with long-term cost.

FAQs

Which loan is better?
The loan with the lower total repayment is generally better. Always consider total interest, tenure, and EMI together to make a well-informed decision.
Does tenure affect total cost?
Yes. Extending the tenure lowers EMI but increases total interest, making the loan more expensive overall.
Should I only look at interest rate?
No. Interest rate alone is insufficient. Total repayment, tenure, and EMI all must be evaluated to determine the best option.
Is lower EMI always better?
Not necessarily. Lower EMI may reduce monthly pressure but increase total cost due to longer tenure.
Can I compare different types of loans?
Yes. This calculator allows comparing different loans, including personal loans, home loans, or car loans, to find the most cost-effective option.
How often should I compare loans?
Whenever you consider a new loan, it is advisable to run a comparison to ensure you choose the most financially beneficial option.
Will prepayment impact the comparison?
Yes. Prepayment reduces total interest and can significantly change which loan is cheaper overall. Consider prepayment options when comparing.
