Retirement Stress Test

This calculator helps you test whether your retirement corpus is robust enough to handle expenses, inflation, and investment fluctuations over time. It subtracts your yearly withdrawals while compounding your savings to provide a realistic picture of financial stability in retirement.

Why a Stress Test Matters

Many retirees underestimate how withdrawals, inflation, and market volatility affect their corpus. This stress test simulates real-world scenarios, helping you adjust savings, withdrawals, or investment strategies proactively.

How to Use the Calculator

FAQs

What is a retirement stress test?
A retirement stress test simulates withdrawals, inflation, and investment growth to check whether your corpus lasts through retirement.
How do I interpret the results?
If the final corpus remains positive and grows, your plan is sustainable. If it decreases or becomes zero, adjustments are needed in savings, withdrawals, or investment returns.
Can I adjust my withdrawal amount?
Yes. Lowering withdrawals or increasing corpus can significantly improve retirement sustainability.
What rate of return should I assume?
Use a conservative estimate, typically 6–10% depending on your portfolio, to avoid overestimating growth.
How often should I run a stress test?
Review annually or after major market events to ensure your retirement plan remains on track.
Does inflation affect the test?
Yes. You can manually increase withdrawals over time to account for inflation, or use linked inflation calculators for more accuracy.
Should I invest aggressively for higher growth?
Balanced portfolios often work best. Aggressive investing may increase growth but also exposes you to high volatility risk.
What if I start late?
If you start retirement savings late, you may need higher contributions or lower withdrawals to maintain sustainability.
