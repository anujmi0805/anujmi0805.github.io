What is PPF?

PPF (Public Provident Fund) is a secure government-backed investment scheme that encourages disciplined savings. It offers attractive tax-free interest rates and is ideal for individuals seeking a long-term, safe investment option to accumulate a substantial corpus over time.

Why PPF Matters

PPF allows investors to grow their wealth securely, while enjoying tax benefits under Section 80C. It is a perfect option for risk-averse individuals who value steady growth, financial discipline, and long-term financial planning.

FAQs

Is PPF completely tax-free?
Yes, contributions, interest, and maturity proceeds are fully tax-exempt under the EEE category, offering high efficiency for long-term savings.
What is the PPF lock-in period?
PPF accounts have a standard lock-in of 15 years, encouraging disciplined long-term wealth accumulation.
Can I make partial withdrawals?
Partial withdrawals are allowed from the 7th financial year onward under specific conditions, providing some liquidity while maintaining growth.
Can I extend my PPF account after maturity?
Yes, extensions are possible in blocks of 5 years beyond the initial 15-year term to continue earning tax-free returns.
Is there a maximum yearly contribution?
The current maximum contribution is ₹1.5 lakh per financial year, allowing careful planning of your investments.
Can a minor open a PPF account?
Yes, a parent or guardian can open and manage a PPF account for a minor, promoting early financial literacy and planning.
Does the interest rate change every year?
Yes, the government revises the interest rate quarterly. While small fluctuations occur, PPF remains a reliable investment for long-term growth.
Can I use PPF for my child’s education or marriage?
Yes, PPF is an excellent tool to save for major life goals such as education or marriage, ensuring tax-free, secure growth.
What happens if I miss a yearly contribution?
Missing a contribution only requires a minimal penalty, and the account continues, making it flexible for unexpected financial situations.
