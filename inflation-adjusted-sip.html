What is Inflation Adjusted SIP?

Inflation-adjusted SIP reflects the real purchasing power of your investments. While nominal returns may appear high, inflation gradually erodes the value of money over time. This calculator helps you understand what your future wealth actually means in today's terms.

Why It Matters

Ignoring inflation can lead to overestimating your financial goals. A return of 12% may seem strong, but if inflation is 6%, your real return is significantly lower. Understanding this gap is essential for accurate financial planning.

Example

If your SIP grows to ₹10 lakh in the future, inflation may reduce its real value to ₹6–7 lakh in today’s purchasing power. This highlights why inflation-adjusted planning is critical.

FAQs

What is inflation adjusted return?
It represents the real return after subtracting the impact of inflation from nominal gains, helping you assess the true value of your investments.
Why should I consider inflation?
Inflation reduces purchasing power, meaning your future money will buy less than today. Ignoring it can cause overestimation of savings and retirement planning.
Can SIP beat inflation?
Yes, equity-based SIPs historically outperform inflation over long periods, making them an effective tool to preserve and grow wealth.
What is a good real return?
A real return of 3–5% above inflation is generally considered healthy for long-term wealth accumulation.
Should I increase my SIP over time?
Yes, gradually increasing your SIP helps counter inflation and amplifies your long-term financial growth.
Does inflation affect long-term goals?
Absolutely. It significantly impacts goals like retirement, education funds, and wealth planning, so planning with inflation in mind is essential.
How to plan investments with inflation?
Always calculate inflation-adjusted returns, monitor portfolio performance, and revise your goals periodically to stay on track.
Can inflation impact emergency funds?
Yes, even emergency funds can lose value over time if inflation is ignored, so allocate and review them carefully.
Is it important for all types of SIPs?
Yes, whether equity, debt, or hybrid SIPs, adjusting for inflation ensures realistic growth expectations across your portfolio.
