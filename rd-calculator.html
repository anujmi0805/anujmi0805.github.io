FAQs

1. What is an RD?
A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a savings instrument where you deposit a fixed amount every month for a predetermined period and earn interest. It is ideal for disciplined savings and achieving financial goals over time.
2. How is RD interest calculated?
Interest is calculated on a quarterly compounding basis in most banks. This means every quarter, the interest is computed on your current balance, helping your savings grow steadily.
3. Can I withdraw my RD before maturity?
Premature withdrawal is possible but may attract penalties and reduced interest. It’s recommended to plan your RD for the entire duration to maximize returns.
4. What is the maturity value?
Maturity value is the total amount you receive at the end of your RD term, including your principal investment and the interest earned.
5. How do I choose the RD amount and duration?
Choose an amount you can comfortably invest each month and a duration aligned with your financial goals. Use the calculator to test different scenarios and plan wisely.
6. Is RD better than a savings account?
RD generally offers higher interest rates than savings accounts, while maintaining safety and predictability, making it suitable for medium-term savings goals.
7. Can NRIs invest in RD?
Yes, NRIs can invest in RDs, but they must follow banking regulations and may have slightly different interest rates and rules compared to resident investors.
