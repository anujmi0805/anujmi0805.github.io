Sequence of Return Risk

Sequence of return risk refers to the danger that the order of investment returns in the early years of retirement can significantly impact your portfolio’s longevity. Even with the same average returns, early losses can deplete your savings faster than later losses. Understanding this concept is crucial for planning a sustainable retirement.

Understanding Sequence of Return Risk

The sequence in which your investment returns occur can dramatically influence the sustainability of your retirement corpus. Early losses may force withdrawals from a reduced base, while early gains provide a buffer against future market volatility.

Why It Matters

Ignoring sequence of return risk can jeopardize your financial independence. By simulating various scenarios, you can design withdrawal strategies and portfolio allocations that reduce the risk of running out of money in retirement.

FAQs

What is sequence of return risk?
It is the risk that the order of your investment returns affects your portfolio sustainability, especially during early retirement years.
How can I reduce this risk?
Diversifying investments, maintaining a buffer, and planning flexible withdrawals can help mitigate sequence risk.
Does market volatility increase this risk?
Yes, higher market volatility in the early retirement period increases the impact of sequence risk.
Is this only for retirees?
No, anyone withdrawing from investments or living off a portfolio faces sequence risk, though it is most critical in retirement.
Can safe withdrawal rates protect me?
Safe withdrawal rates help, but they should be applied flexibly, accounting for market performance year by year.
How often should I simulate scenarios?
Ideally, at least annually or whenever major market changes occur.
What happens if returns are negative in early years?
Early negative returns can significantly reduce your corpus, highlighting the importance of conservative early withdrawal strategies.
Does this impact all retirement portfolios equally?
The effect varies depending on asset allocation, withdrawal rate, and investment horizon. Personalized planning is key.
