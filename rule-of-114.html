Rule of 114

The Rule of 114 is a simple investment heuristic used to estimate the time required to triple an investment at a fixed annual return. By dividing 114 by the annual interest rate, you can approximate how many years it will take for your money to triple.

Calculate Tripling Time

Years to Triple Investment

14.25 Years

Understanding the Rule of 114

Divide 114 by the expected annual return (in percent) to estimate how long it will take to triple your investment. For example, an 8% return will take roughly 114 ÷ 8 = 14.25 years.

Applications of the Rule

It helps investors quickly understand long-term growth scenarios for fixed deposits, mutual funds, and other compounding investments. Planning becomes easier and decisions more disciplined.

Limitations

This is an approximation and assumes a constant annual return. Market fluctuations may cause slight differences, but the formula is a quick and practical guide for financial literacy.

FAQs

What is the Rule of 114?
A quick formula to estimate how long it takes to triple your investment based on annual returns.
How is it different from Rule of 72?
Rule of 72 estimates doubling time; Rule of 114 estimates tripling time.
Can I use it for any investment?
Yes, including fixed deposits, mutual funds, and savings accounts with compounding interest.
Does it account for market fluctuations?
No, it assumes a constant annual return.
Is this for short-term investments?
Best for medium to long-term investments due to compounding effect.
Why use the Rule of 114?
It provides a quick estimate to plan long-term investment goals without complex calculations.
Can it replace financial planning?
No, it's a mental shortcut. Comprehensive planning is always recommended.
