Understanding Real Return

Real return reflects the true increase in the value of your investments after accounting for inflation. It helps investors distinguish between apparent gains and actual gains in purchasing power, ensuring smarter financial planning.

Importance in Financial Planning

Ignoring inflation can mislead your wealth projections. Real return enables better decision-making for long-term goals like retirement, children's education, and wealth preservation.

How to Interpret

For example, a 12% nominal return against a 6% inflation rate yields a 6% real return. This means your money grows in real terms, preserving and expanding its purchasing power over time.

Applications

Real return is fundamental in evaluating investments, comparing asset classes, planning systematic investments, and understanding the impact of inflation on your financial goals.

FAQs

Is real return always lower than nominal return?
Yes, because inflation reduces purchasing power, real return is usually lower than nominal returns, showing the actual gain in wealth.
Why is inflation important in investing?
Inflation erodes the value of money. Considering it helps you understand how much your investments are truly growing in terms of purchasing power.
How do I calculate real return manually?
Real return = ((1 + nominal return) / (1 + inflation)) - 1. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage.
What is considered a good real return?
A consistent real return of 3–5% or more is generally healthy for long-term wealth creation.
Can real return be negative?
Yes, if inflation exceeds the nominal return, the real return becomes negative, indicating a loss in purchasing power.
How does real return affect retirement planning?
It ensures your retirement corpus grows in actual value, allowing you to maintain lifestyle without being affected by inflation.
Is real return the same for all asset classes?
No. Stocks, bonds, and real estate may show different real returns due to varying exposure to inflation and market risks.
How often should I review real returns?
Review annually or whenever major financial changes occur to ensure your portfolio aligns with your real growth expectations.
