What is XIRR?

XIRR, or Extended Internal Rate of Return, is a financial metric that calculates the annualized return of investments when cash flows occur at irregular intervals. Unlike simple CAGR, XIRR considers each inflow and outflow individually, providing a more accurate measure for real-world investments, such as SIPs, staggered deposits, and partial withdrawals.

How XIRR Works

XIRR evaluates each cash flow separately and calculates a single rate that equates the net present value of all cash flows to zero. This makes it particularly useful for portfolios with multiple transactions over time. Investors rely on XIRR to compare real investment growth against targets or benchmarks.

Why XIRR Matters

XIRR is critical for investors who make irregular contributions or withdrawals. Unlike CAGR, which assumes uniform growth, XIRR adjusts for the timing and amount of each cash flow, providing a precise annualized return that reflects real performance.

Applications of XIRR

XIRR is widely used in mutual funds, stock investments, retirement planning, and portfolio analysis. Whether comparing lump sum investments versus SIPs or evaluating staggered returns from multiple accounts, XIRR offers a clear and reliable metric to track actual gains.

Example

Consider an investor who invests ₹50,000 monthly in a mutual fund for 12 months, with varying dates of deposits and partial withdrawals. Using XIRR, they can calculate the true annualized growth rate of their portfolio, rather than relying on approximate methods like CAGR.

FAQs

What is the difference between XIRR and CAGR?
CAGR assumes uniform growth, while XIRR accounts for irregular cash flows, providing a more accurate annualized return for real investments.
Can XIRR be negative?
Yes, if the portfolio experiences net losses or withdrawals exceeding gains, XIRR will reflect a negative return.
Is XIRR useful for SIPs?
Absolutely. SIPs involve multiple deposits over time, and XIRR accurately calculates the effective annual growth rate of such investments.
Does XIRR consider timing of cash flows?
Yes, XIRR adjusts for the exact date of each inflow or outflow, which makes it highly precise for irregular investments.
Can XIRR replace other metrics like ROI?
XIRR complements other metrics but is superior when cash flows are irregular. ROI gives simple percentage return without time adjustment.
How do I calculate XIRR manually?
Manual calculation involves solving the internal rate of return formula iteratively, which is complex. Most investors use spreadsheet software or XIRR calculators.
Is XIRR reliable for long-term investments?
Yes, XIRR provides an accurate measure of long-term performance when contributions and withdrawals occur at irregular intervals.
Which investments should I use XIRR for?
XIRR is best for mutual funds, SIPs, dividend reinvestments, real estate cash flows, and any investment with multiple transactions at different times.
